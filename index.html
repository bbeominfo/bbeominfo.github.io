01 — About

Who I am

I'm a graduate student pursuing dual master's degrees in Computational Science & Engineering and Bioinformatics at Georgia Tech, where I work at the Bio-MIBLab on machine learning systems for biomedical data.

My research focuses on applying large language models and deep learning to complex biological problems — from multimodal disease risk prediction to gene embedding and multi-agent AI architectures. I'm passionate about building systems that are not only accurate but interpretable and clinically useful.

Before GT, I gained research experience at KAIST's Single-Cell Medical Genomics Lab and worked as an intern at Soongsil University and Insilicogen, a bioinformatics company. I hold a Bachelor's in Biomedical Engineering and Computer Art from Chung-ang University, Seoul.
