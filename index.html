About

Who I am

I'm a graduate student pursuing dual master's degrees in Computational Science & Engineering and Bioinformatics at Georgia Tech, where I work at the Bio-MIBLab on machine learning systems for biomedical data.

My research focuses on applying large language models and deep learning to complex biological problems — from multimodal disease risk prediction to gene embedding and multi-agent AI architectures. I care about building systems that are accurate, interpretable, and clinically deployable.

Before GT, I conducted research at KAIST's Single-Cell Medical Genomics Lab and interned at Soongsil University and Insilicogen. I hold a Bachelor's in Biomedical Engineering and Computer Art from Chung-ang University, Seoul.
